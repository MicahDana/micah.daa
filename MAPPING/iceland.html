Iceland

Land of Fire and Ice

Iceland is renowned for its dramatic landscapes that combine volcanic terrain, glaciers, waterfalls, and geothermal wonders. One of the most awe-inspiring natural phenomena here is the aurora borealis—an ethereal light show dancing across the night sky.

I was captivated by the raw beauty and solitude of Iceland. Its otherworldly scenery, the sound of crashing waterfalls, and the quiet nights illuminated by the northern lights evoke a sense of wonder and tranquility.

It is special to me because I like night lights and the aurora borealis is a magical one.

How to Get There

Most international flights arrive at Keflavík International Airport near Reykjavík. From there, you can rent a car or take a bus to explore Iceland’s diverse regions—from the Golden Circle to the remote highlands. Be sure to check the weather, as conditions can be unpredictable.

Other Places to Visit

Besides the mesmerizing aurora, Iceland offers countless natural wonders: the Blue Lagoon, rugged Westfjords, stunning waterfalls like Gullfoss, and the dramatic black sand beaches of Vík. Each region offers its own unique charm.
